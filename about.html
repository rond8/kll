KLL
Kolehiyo ng Lungsod ng Lipa

Formerly: Lipa City Public College

Description

It was the dream of the Lipa City government under former Mayor Ruben L. Umali to have a government school that could cater to poor but deserving students. The dream came true with the help of the SIPAG LAKAS movement which paved the way for the establishment of Lipa City Public College.

The idea was to create an institution called Lipa City Public Colleges with three components: a science high school as a feeder school to the country’s engineering and science colleges and universities; a general high school for the students of the Poblacion so they would not have to go to faraway barangay schools; and a technical-vocational school for high school graduates who could not afford the cost of studying at private technical-vocational schools.

At the time the idea was conceived, Lipa City already had the Lipa City Science High School and the General High School. What was missing was the technical- vocational school. As a start, the Science High School offered post-secondary courses such as Associate in Computer Science and Office Management with Computer Application.

Lipa City Science High School with these post-secondary courses was renamed Lipa City Science and Technology Institute. This institution was later integrated with the General High School, forming the Lipa City Integrated School.

In 1991, Hon. Ralph Recto was elected Congressman of the Fourth District of Batangas to which Lipa City belongs. The mayor at that time, Mayor Ruben L. Umali, requested the honorable congressman for the conversion of Lipa City Integrated School into a state college.

However, the legislative process involved in establishing a state college was long and tedious. Mayor Umali felt that poor students who could not pay high tuition fees in private tertiary schools urgently needed a college. He exerted efforts to establish a government-run college to serve poor and deserving students.

In January 1994, a project proposal for the establishment of a community college was prepared and submitted to the Sangguniang Panglungsod of Lipa City. On February 14, 1994, through Ordinance No. 04, Series of 1994, the community college of Lipa was established.

On March 14, 1994, another ordinance was approved (Ordinance No. 94-08), amending Ordinance No. 04-94 by changing the term “establishing” to “converting,” thus the community college was converted to Lipa City Public Colleges.

With Special Ordinance No. 04-96, the name Lipa City Public Colleges was changed to Lipa Public College. On June 15, 1998, Special Ordinance No. 13-98 was approved prescribing the rules and regulations for the operation of Lipa City Public College.

PHP 1,700,000.00 was appropriated for the initial operation of the college. The legal basis for its operation was secured and CHED approved the college permits for the school year 1994–1995, first semester.

By 2002, Lipa City Public College had grown significantly. From a single building with four classrooms, additional buildings were constructed: one with twenty classrooms and another with five classrooms. The old building that once housed administrative offices was converted into a two-storey building with air-conditioned offices.

The National Food Authority (NFA) building, previously partitioned into 12 classrooms, was converted into the college gym. These developments were made possible through the support of Hon. Senator Ralph G. Recto and the local government under Hon. Vilma Santos-Recto.

The college served 3,969 students not only from Lipa City but also from nearby towns and cities. This increase in enrollment showed the strong demand for free college education.

In November 2009, under former Mayor Oscar "OCA" L. Gozos, the campus was relocated to Brgy. Marawoy-Dagatan, Lipa City and renamed Kolehiyo ng Lungsod ng Lipa.

Under Mayor Meynard A. Sabili, the college now serves more than 5,000 students from Lipa City and nearby municipalities. This overwhelming increase in enrollment demonstrates the importance of accessible and affordable higher education.

Kolehiyo ng Lungsod ng Lipa is committed to the four-fold mission of instruction, research, extension service, and employability.

With its noble task of serving economically underprivileged youth with free but high-quality education, KLL continues to live up to its vision, mission, and goals.
